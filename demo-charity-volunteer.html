NDIS Code of Conduct and Practice Standards

NDIS Code of Conduct

The NDIS Code of Conduct sets the standard for ethical and professional behaviour for all providers and workers delivering NDIS supports and services. It ensures that participants receive respectful, quality, and safe services from registered providers.

What are the NDIS Practice Standards?

The NDIS Practice Standards establish benchmarks for providers to evaluate their performance and demonstrate the quality and safety of the services they offer to NDIS participants. These standards, alongside the NDIS Code of Conduct, help participants understand the level of service they should expect from their providers.

The standards are outlined in the National Disability Insurance Scheme (Provider Registration and Practice Standards) Rules 2018 and are supported by the National Disability Insurance Scheme (Quality Indicators) Guidelines 2018, which detail the outcomes and quality indicators for compliance.

Core and Supplementary Modules

The NDIS Practice Standards include a core module and supplementary modules tailored to the specific supports and services delivered by NDIS providers.

Core Modules:

  • Rights and Responsibility for Participants: Ensures participants are treated with dignity, respect, and informed of their rights.
  • Governance and Operational Management: Focuses on the governance structure, management systems, and financial and operational capacity of providers.
  • Provision of Supports: Ensures that supports are delivered safely and effectively.
  • Support Provision Environment: Ensures that the environment where supports are delivered is safe, accessible, and suitable for participants.

Supplementary Modules Include:

  • High-Intensity Daily Personal Activities
  • Specialist Behaviour Support
  • Implementing Behaviour Support Plans
  • Early Childhood Supports
  • Specialised Support Coordination
  • Specialist Disability Accommodation

NDIS Quality & Safeguards Commission

The NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission works to improve the quality and safety of NDIS services by collaborating with participants and providers. Participants have the right to express concerns or complaints about their supports, which can lead to service improvements.

How to Make a Complaint

Participants have the right to make complaints about the services they receive. If you have concerns about your NDIS supports, it’s encouraged to first discuss them with your service provider. All registered providers are required to have a complaints management system.

NDIS Pricing Arrangements and Limits 2023-24

The NDIS Pricing Arrangements and Price Limits guide, released annually on 1 July by the National Disability Insurance Agency (NDIA), sets the maximum costs for NDIS supports and services.
